Fertiliser Notes

A feeding guide that actually makes sense

N-P-K ratios and feeding frequency by plant type, plus how to spot a nutrient deficiency.

N 12 – P 4 – K 8

🥬 Leafy vegetables & lawns

High nitrogen drives leafy green growth. Feed every 4–6 weeks through the growing season.

N 5 – P 10 – K 10

🌹 Flowering & fruiting (roses, tomatoes)

Higher phosphorus & potassium supports flowers and fruit. Feed every 3–4 weeks in season.

N 10 – P 10 – K 10 (½ strength)

🪴 Indoor houseplants

Balanced, diluted liquid feed monthly in spring/summer. Stop feeding entirely over winter.

Low-phosphorus blend

🌿 Australian natives

Many natives are phosphorus-sensitive — use a dedicated low-P native fertiliser, applied lightly in spring and autumn.

N 8 – P 3 – K 9 + Mg

🍋 Citrus trees

Feed in early spring, late spring and autumn. Added magnesium prevents the yellowing common in potted citrus.

Compost + slow-release

🌻 General garden beds

A balanced slow-release granular feed at planting, topped up with compost twice a year, suits most mixed beds.
